Santa Ana Volcano

Dare Youself to Reach the Summit

Test your endurance by reaching the summit of the Ilamatepec volcano, also called Santa Ana volcano. If you make it, you will have some of the most impressive views of the area, with the turquoise waters of Lake Coatepeque in the background.

Located in the department of Santa Ana, the colossus has an altitude of 2,381 meters above sea level, the highest in El Salvador. Its last eruptions occurred in 2005.

It is part of the Los Volcanes Complex in the Apaneca mountain range, within a tropical mountainous cloud forest, in a coffee-growing region about 65 kilometers west of the capital. The main activities of the region are agriculture and tourism, so you will not regret the scenery you will see from the road, with stalls selling fruits that characterize the area.

Once you start climbing the volcano the experience with the flora and fauna will be incredible, providing you with a cool and delicious climate. And once at the top of this imposing colossus you will have spectacular views of the Izalco volcano and the lake of Coatepeque.

The ascent is medium level (5/10), with weather that varies. Recommendations for hikers include comfortable clothing, water, sunglasses, sunscreen, hat or cap, and footwear suitable for mountaineering or hiking.

Entrance to the Complex is $1.50 for Salvadorans, foreigners pay $3.

Children under 6 years old and over 60 years old are free.

Hours of operation are from 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.
